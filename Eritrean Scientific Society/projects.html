Abstract

As any, transportation system whether it is air born, land or even water, should be on an organized and safety approved system. The transportation system needs to obtain concrete data in order to improve its overall efficiency both organization and customer wise. A vehicle tracking is the basic requirement in all fleet management systems. In fleet management system we aim to improve the quality and efficiency of the organization by identifying the real-time locations of their vehicles on a map. In this project we try to overcome the challenges of urban transportation and develop an effective approach to intelligently manage the vehicles of an organization with an efficient vehicle tracking system. This system is designed and implemented for tracking the movement of any GPS receiver equipped vehicle from any location at any time as long as you have an internet connection. It makes good use of the popular technology that combines GSM and GPS application with a microcontroller. This will be easy to make and inexpensive compared to others. The designed in-vehicle device works using Global Positioning System (GPS) and sends the vehicle location to the central controlling unit via satellite. Once these real-time locations are mapped on the server, the Global System for Mobile Communication (GSM) can be used to communicate with the vehicle driver and turn off his engine if he is on unauthorized place. A microcontroller is used to control the GPS and GSM modules. This overall system is easy to deploy and provide effective management tool to transportation authorities for optimal utilization of present resources with ease-of-use for the commuter and most importantly to the monitoring and controlling authorities. In addition this system can also be integrated with different technologies for additional features and future developments.

Introduction

The effective transportation system leads to the effective movement of goods and people, which significantly contribute to the quality of life in every society. In the Heart of every economic and social development, there is always a transportation system. Therefore rapidly increasing vehicle population in Eritrea due to increase in population and economic upturn lays a very high complex burden on metropolitan traffic management. These mass transit facilities are operating with manual method of fleet management which in turn also affects the vehicle’s organization by decreasing their controlling ability. Intelligent technology based tracking & navigation system is still not used in such transportation. This results in more effort to manage such a large system and dependency on manual methods which could be prone to error. This proposed paper is an attempt to design a tracking unit that uses the Global positioning system (GPS) to determine the latitude and longitude coordinates of a vehicle along the path. This information will be gathered by the Head Quarter with the help of internet from satellite and it will be mapped. And with the help of Global system of mobile communication (GSM) we can contact the driver. It also helps the Central controlling authority to track down the fleets with its real-time location on map to manage it precisely and optimally. In this paper, a transportation management system is developed for enhancing transportation services based on combination of GPS and GSM systems. GPS is used in positioning the device while GSM is used as communication link between different modules.

Methodology
GPS Technology

A GPS tracking device is a reliable way to observe an object in motion. It makes the necessary details such as location and time information irrespective of the weather conditions available, anywhere on or near the earth. The Passenger Information Systems exist in various cities that use GPS (Global Positioning System) systems for bus tracking. This technology demands line-of-sight between the receiver and the satellites. Ignoring this condition makes the GPS signal attenuate. This limitation makes GPS unsuitable for bus tracking systems inside a sub terrestrial bus station. These two major drawbacks can be overcome by the proposed system as it involves RF transceiver that has a communication range up to 1000m and demands neither line-of-sight communication nor satellite communication.

The methodology of this project can be classified into two

1. The vehicle module
2. The head quarter’s module


The Head Quarter's Module

A GPS receiver is placed in each vehicle. This GPS receiver receives signal from at least three GPS satellites orbiting around the world. Minimum three satellites are needed so the receiver would know its two dimensional location (longitude and latitude). We can also get the longitude latitude and altitude using four satellites. There might be more than four satellites but it only increases the accuracy of positioning. This location data is then transmitted to a server. The server is a computer or any other device with an internet access and is placed in head quarter (control room of the fleet manager). Each receiver is separately registered with different address hence all are displayed on a map of the server with its specific address. The attendant who is on the server is then able to check each vehicle position. As explained in our objective, if the fleet is of police department or ambulance the attendant may order to go to the nearest.

The vehicle module

There are 4 main parts that is the GSM module, the GPS tracker, the microcontroller and finally the LCD. The main purpose of this module is to track the vehicle and lay out instruction from the head quarter’s module through the LCD screen.

When the GPS receiver calculates the position of the vehicle with the aid of satellites, the exact pin point of the car will be located. This location will be sent to the head quarter’s receiver and the vehicles exact position will be detected at the server. The GPS system works via satellite but unlike this system the data sent to the LCD 16x2 screen is sent through GSM system. If the vehicle is outside any of its original trajectory the head quarter controller will send a warning through GSM to the LCD. But this screen can also be used as a way to assign the vehicles’ route. In vehicles of rental cars or any other company vehicles in addition to the GSM receiver, ON/OFF circuit will be added to the ignition part of the engine. This part or device will be activated through GSM signal if and only if the vehicle is out of its jurisdiction in any way and the driver ignores the warning given by the official. This device can be used to contain rental cars with in their parameters.

Limitations

As any project this particular project has limitations as well. The projects limitations can be divided into two main factors.
1. The first factor is that the project is mainly dependent on a GSM connection system. For a GSM connection to work properly different factors are needed like a proper location for a good signal. Locations like trenches and mountain can block the signal and lower the efficiency of the communication. A proper signal must be available for the system to work properly. The signal towers responsible for the area must be uninterrupted and fully functional. With the present power limitation in Eritrea sometimes the vehicle needs to find a good reception area for us to shut off the engine.
2. The second factor is internet connection which is needed for this system to work. This is a very important part if we are going to get the instantaneous location of a vehicle. But considering the benefits of this system, paying for a good internet connection is a small price to pay.
